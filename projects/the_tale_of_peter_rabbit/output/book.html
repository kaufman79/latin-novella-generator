Fābula dē Petrō Cunīculō

The Tale of Peter Rabbit

Peter cum familiā sub arbore magnā habitābat.

Peter lived with his family under a big tree.

1

Māter cunīculōs parvōs vestīvit. Peter tunicam caeruleam gerēbat.

Mother dressed the little rabbits. Peter wore his blue jacket.

2

"Nōlīte īre in hortum Dominī McGregor!" inquit Māter.

"Do not go into Mr. McGregor's garden!" said Mother.

3

Tum Māter cum corbulā abiit.

Then Mother went out with her basket.

4

Cunīculī bonī mōra in viā carpēbant.

The good rabbits picked blackberries in the lane.

5

Sed Peter rēctā ad hortum cucurrit!

But Peter ran straight to the garden!

6

Peter lactūcam ēdit, et fabās, et rādīcēs.

Peter ate lettuce and beans and radishes.

7

Nimis ēdit! Venter eius dolēbat.

He ate too much. His stomach hurt.

8

Dominus McGregor eum vīdit! "Cōnsiste, fūr!"

Mr. McGregor saw him! "Stop, thief!"

9

Peter valdē perterritus erat. Cucurrit et cucurrit!

Peter was very scared. He ran and ran!

10

Tunica Petrī in rēte haesit. Haerēbat!

Peter's jacket got caught in a net. He was stuck!

11

Passerēs ad eum volāvērunt. "Perge! Perge!" pipiāvērunt.

Sparrows flew to him. "Keep trying!" they chirped.

12

Peter sē līberāvit, sed tunicam et calceōs āmīsit.

Peter pulled free, but he lost his jacket and shoes.

13

Peter in vās aquārium sē abdidit! Aqua frīgida erat!

Peter hid in a watering can full of cold water!

14

Dominus McGregor ubīque Petrum quaerēbat.

Mr. McGregor looked everywhere for Peter.

15

Peter fūrtim exiit — et trēs ollās dēiēcit. Fragor!

Peter sneaked out and knocked over three flowerpots. Crash!

16

Peter errābat. Portam invenīre nōn poterat.

Peter was lost. He could not find the gate.

17

Mūrem parvam rogāvit, sed ea adiuvāre nōn potuit.

He asked a little mouse for help, but she could not help him.

18

Peter fēlem albam vīdit. Procul ab eā mānsit.

Peter saw a white cat. He stayed away from her.

19

Tum Peter portam tandem vīdit!

Then Peter saw the gate at last!

20

Ad portam cucurrit et sub eā sē pressit. Līber!

He ran to the gate and squeezed under it. Free!

21

Tunica Petrī formīdō in hortō facta est.

Peter's jacket became a scarecrow in the garden.

22

Peter domum tōtam viam cucurrit. Valdē fessus erat.

Peter ran all the way home. He was very tired.

23

Māter Petrum in lectum posuit et potum chamomillae dedit.

Mother put Peter to bed with chamomile tea.

24

Sed cunīculī bonī pānem et lac ad cēnam habēbant.

But the good rabbits had bread and milk for supper.
