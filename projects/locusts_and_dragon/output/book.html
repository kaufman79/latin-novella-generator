locusts and dragon

Multae cicādae ūnō diē taedēbant. Nihil novum erat.

Many cicadas were bored one day. Nothing was new.

1

Itaque omnēs ad oppidum proximum volāvērunt.

So they all flew to the nearby town.

2

Ibī hominēs timidi et trīstēs erant.

There, the people were afraid and sad.

3

Dracō eōs terrēbat. Habitābat in monte prope oppidum. Caverna erat magna et obscūra.

A dragon scared them. It lived in a mountain near the town. The cave was large and dark.

4

Cicādae inter sē dīxērunt: “Eāmus! Dracōnem vincāmus!”

The cicadas said to each other: 'Let’s go! Let’s defeat the dragon!'

5

Itaque omnēs ad montem volāvērunt. Ventus fortis erat, sed nōn timuērunt.

So they all flew to the mountain. The wind was strong, but they were not afraid.

6

Cicādae intrāvērunt cavernam. Tenebrās vīdērunt et nihil aliud.

The cicadas entered the cave. They saw darkness and nothing else.

7

Per obscūra forāmina et longa itinera ambulāvērunt.

They walked through dark tunnels and long paths.

8

Tandem invēnērunt cubīle dracōnis. Dracō dormiēbat super acervum aurī.

At last they found the dragon’s lair. The dragon was sleeping on a pile of gold.

9

Aurum et gemmae fulgēbant. Dracō ruber erat et dentēs magnī erant eī.

The gold and gems sparkled. The dragon was red and had large teeth.

10

Cicādae super dracōnem volāvērunt. Eum pungēre temptāvērunt, sed frūstrā.

The cicadas flew above the dragon. They tried to sting it, but in vain.

11

Dracō excitātus est et īrātus factus est. Rugīvit valdē et ālās mōvit.

The dragon woke up and became angry. It roared loudly and moved its wings.

12

Per antrum volāvit, mordēns et ungues vibrāns. Multās cicādās occīdit.

It flew through the cave, biting and swinging its claws. It killed many cicadas.

13

Ceterae cicādae tremēbant et consilium quaesīvērunt.

The remaining cicadas trembled and sought a plan.

14

Antōnius, una cicāda fortis, dīxit: “Strīdāmus omnēs simul!”

Antonius, one brave cicada, said: 'Let’s all buzz together!'

15

Antōnius prīmus strīdit. Deinde omnēs cicādae fortiter strīdēbant.

Antonius buzzed first. Then all the cicadas buzzed loudly.

16

Sonus magnus tōtam cavernam implēvit. Aurēs dracōnis doluērunt.

A great sound filled the whole cave. The dragon’s ears hurt.

17

Dracō īnsānīvit et sine mente volāvit. In parietem cavernae volāvit et cecidit.

The dragon went mad and flew without sense. It crashed into the cave wall and fell.

18

Dracō mortuus est. Cicādae gāvīsae sunt.

The dragon was dead. The cicadas rejoiced.

19

Ad oppidum rediērunt et strīdēbant: “Dracō mortuus est!”

They returned to the town and buzzed: 'The dragon is dead!'

20

Hominēs laetī erant et grātiās ēgērunt.

The people were happy and gave thanks.

21

Aurum cēpērunt et inter sē dīvīsērunt.

They took the gold and shared it among themselves.

22

Omnēs laetī erant. Cicādae carmen laetum strīdēbant.

Everyone was happy. The cicadas buzzed a joyful song.

23
