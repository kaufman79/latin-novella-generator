## Title

**Machina Parva Quae Potuit** — "The Little Engine That Could"

- *machina* is standard for "engine/machine" — well attested in classical and late Latin.
- *quae potuit* — relative clause, "which could/was able." Perfect tense is correct: the title refers to the accomplished feat.
- Alternative considered: *Machinula Quae Potuit* (with diminutive) — rejected as less natural and *machinula* is poorly attested.

### Page 15

- *dēsīderant* — "desire/need." Present tense — the children's need is ongoing. Correct.
- **"Possum, putō"** — THE REFRAIN. "I can, I think." Analysis:

  - *putō* — "I think / I reckon." First person singular present. Colloquial, natural register — exactly right for a picture book. Not *crēdō* (too theological) or *opinor* (too formal).

- Caps for second repetition shows growing determination.
- Word count: 9 (including dialogue). Good.
