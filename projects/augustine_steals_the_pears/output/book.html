Augustīnus et Pirus

Augustine steals the pears

Augustīnus erat puer laetus.

Augustine was a happy boy.

1

Amābat currere et rīdēre cum amīcīs.

He loved to run and laugh with friends.

2

Ūnā nocte, lūna clāra fulsit.

One night, the moon shone brightly.

3

Pirus stābat trans saepem.

A pear tree stood beyond the fence.

4

Pira erant rotunda et lucida.

The pears were round and shiny.

5

"Capiāmus pira!" dixērunt amīcī.

"Let’s take the pears!" said the friends.

6

Conscientia Augustīnī susurrāvit, "Nōlī fūrārī."

Augustine’s conscience whispered, "Don’t steal."

7

Sed nōn audīvit.

But he didn’t listen.

8

Tacitē trans saepem iērunt.

Silently, they went over the fence.

9

Arborem agitāvērunt. Pira cecidērunt!

They shook the tree. Pears fell!

10

Frūctūs abstulērunt et fūgērunt,

They took the fruit and fled,

11

et rīdentēs sub stēllīs.

and laughing under the stars.

12

Sed pira nōn erant bona.

But the pears were not good.

13

Pira porcīs iēcērunt.

They threw the pears to the pigs.

14

Porcī erant laetī. Augustīnus nōn erat laetus.

The pigs were happy. Augustine was not.

15

Conscientia eius gravis erat.

His conscience was heavy.

16

Domum iit et cubitum iit.

He went home and to bed.

17

Sed dormīre nōn poterat.

But he couldn’t sleep.

18

Tacitē sēdit et ōrāvit: "Domine meus, doleō fūrārī pira et nōn audīre vōcem interiōrem."

He sat quietly and prayed: "My Lord, I’m sorry for stealing pears and not listening to my inner voice."

19

Augustīnus amōrem Deī in corde sēnsit. Deus eī iamprīdem remīserat.

Augustine felt God’s love in his heart. God had forgiven him long ago.

20

Cum dīcimus, "Mē paenitet, Deus," Deus dīcit, "Remittō tibi!"

When we say, "I’m sorry, God," God says, "I forgive you!"
