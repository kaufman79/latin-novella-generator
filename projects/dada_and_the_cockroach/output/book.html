Fabula de Blatta

Dada and the Cockroach

Cāritās

Nox erat.

It was night.

1

Pater in lectō dormit.

Father sleeps on the couch.

2

Aurēlius in cūnīs dormit.

Aurēlius sleeps in the crib.

3

Māter Iūdam, infantem, in cubiculō lactat.

Mother nurses Judah, the baby, in the bedroom.

4

Subitō blatta super pulvīnum currit!

Suddenly, a cockroach runs across the pillow!

5

Māter exclāmāvit!

Mother screams!

6

Māter telephōnum capit et patrem vocat:

Mother grabs the phone and calls Father:

7

“Pater, blatta est! Venī! Eam necā!”

“Father, there’s a cockroach! Come kill it!”

8

Pater surgit.

Father gets up.

9

Pater calceum capit.

Father grabs a boot.

10

Pater citō ad cubiculum currit.

Father quickly runs to the bedroom.

11

Māter ostium aperit.

Mother opens the door.

12

Māter dīcit: “Ecce blatta!”

Mother says: “Look, the cockroach!”

13

Blatta in cistā pannōrum currit.

The cockroach runs on the basket of diapers.

14

Pater calceum tenet, sed blatta rapida est.

Father holds the boot, but the cockroach is fast.

15

Blatta in culcitam currit.

The cockroach runs onto the changing pad.

16

Pater calceum levat et percutit.

Father lifts the boot and strikes.

17

Ēheu, blatta cadit in solum, nōn mortua!

Oh no, the cockroach falls to the floor, not dead!

18

Blatta in saccō rubrō currit.

The cockroach runs onto the red bag.

19

Pater calceum levat et percutit. Pater blattam calceō percutit!

Father lifts the boot and strikes. Father hits the cockroach with the boot!

20

Pater chartam hygiēnicam petit ut blattam tollat.

Father gets toilet paper to pick up the cockroach.

21

Pater redit, sed blattam nōn videt! Pater saccum movet.

Father returns, but the cockroach is gone! Father moves the bag.

22

Ecce blatta! Pater percutit.

Look, the cockroach! Father strikes.

23

Nunc blatta mortua est.

Now the cockroach is dead.

24

Pater blattam chartā hygiēnicā tollit et dēpōnit in quisquiliās.

Father picks up the cockroach with toilet paper and throws it in the trash can.

25

Māter dīcit: “Grātiās, Pater!” Pater dīcit: “Bene, mea cāra!”

Mother says: “Thank you, Father!” Father says: “You’re welcome, my dear!”

26

Pater in lectum redit et dormit.

Father returns to the couch and sleeps.

27

Blatta nōn redit.

The cockroach does not return.
