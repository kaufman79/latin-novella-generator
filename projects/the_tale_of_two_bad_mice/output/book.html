The Tale of Two Bad Mice

Ecce domus pulchra! Mūrī rubrī et fenestrae albae.

This is a beautiful dollhouse. It has red walls and white windows.

1

Duae pūpae ibi habitābant: Lucinda et Iāna. Iāna coqua erat.

Two dolls lived there: Lucinda and Jane. Jane was the cook.

2

Cibus pulcher in mēnsā erat: perna, piscis, et lībum.

There was pretty food on the table: a ham, a fish, and a pudding.

3

Ōlim māne pūpae exiērunt. Domus tacita erat.

One morning the dolls went out. The house was quiet.

4

Tom Pollex mūs erat. Hunca Munca uxor eius erat.

Tom Thumb was a mouse. Hunca Munca was his wife.

5

Mūrēs ad domum rēpsērunt. Iānuam impulērunt!

The mice crept up to the dollhouse. They pushed the door open!

6

Cēnāculum invēnērunt. Quantae epulae!

They found the dining room. What a feast!

7

Tom Pollex pernam secāre temptāvit. Culter flexus est!

Tom Thumb tried to cut the ham. The knife bent!

8

Hunca Munca quoque temptāvit. Perna dūrissima erat!

Hunca Munca tried too. The ham was very hard!

9

Perna dē pātellā cecidit et sub mēnsam volūta est.

The ham fell off the plate and rolled under the table.

10

Tom Pollex īrātus erat! Cibum batillō frēgit. Gypsum erat!

Tom Thumb was angry! He smashed the food with a shovel. It was all plaster!

11

Piscem in ignem posuērunt. Nōn ārsit!

They put the fish in the fire. It would not burn!

12

Tom Pollex camīnum ascendit. Nūlla fūlīgō!

Tom Thumb went up the chimney. There was no soot!

13

Hunca Munca vāscula invēnit. Sed vacua erant!

Hunca Munca found little cans. But they were empty inside!

14

Mūrēs valdē īrātī erant! Tom Pollex omnia per fenestram iēcit!

The mice were very angry! Tom Thumb threw everything out the window!

15

Hunca Munca culcitam plūmeam domum tulit.

Hunca Munca took the feather bed home to the mouse-hole.

16

Cūnās quoque cēpit, et caveam avium!

She took a cradle and a birdcage too!

17

Tum pūpae domum rediērunt. Quanta ruīna!

Then the dolls came home. What a mess!

18

Sed mūrēs paenituit. Tom Pollex nummum invēnit et prō omnibus quae frēgerat solvit.

But the mice were sorry. Tom Thumb found a coin and paid for everything he broke.

19

Et cotīdiē māne, Hunca Munca domum pūpārum verrit.

And every morning, Hunca Munca sweeps the dollhouse clean.

20
