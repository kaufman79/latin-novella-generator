Jēmima anas erat quae in fundō habitābat.

Jemima was a duck who lived on a farm.

1

Jēmima ōva sua ipsa fovēre volēbat.

Jemima wanted to hatch her own eggs.

2

Sed uxor agricolae ōva semper auferēbat.

But the farmer's wife always took the eggs away.

3

Itaque Jēmima fundum relīquit. Nīdum sēcrētum quaerēbat.

So Jemima left the farm. She was looking for a secret nest.

4

Super collem volāvit, locum quaerēns.

She flew over the hill, looking for a place.

5

In silvam dēscendit. Digitālēs ubīque flōrēbant.

She landed in a wood. Foxgloves bloomed everywhere.

6

Vir elegāns ibi sedēbat, acta diurna legēns.

An elegant gentleman sat there, reading a newspaper.

7

Jēmima eī omnia nārrāvit. Ille attentē audīvit.

Jemima told him everything. He listened carefully.

8

"Locum optimum nōvī," inquit vulpēs, subrīdēns.

"I know a very good place," said the fox, smiling.

9

Tugurium plūmīs plēnum erat. Plūmae mollissimae!

The shed was full of feathers. Very soft feathers!

10

Jēmima novem ōva in nīdō plūmeō posuit.

Jemima laid nine eggs in the feathery nest.

11

Vulpēs valdē comis erat. Cotīdiē cibum dabat.

The fox was very polite. He gave her food every day.

12

Subrīdēbat et dē ōvīs rogābat.

He smiled and asked about the eggs.

13

Tum vulpēs herbās et cēpās poposcit.

Then the fox asked for herbs and onions.

14

Jēmima salviam et cēpās laeta collēgit. Nihil intellegēbat.

Jemima happily gathered sage and onions. She understood nothing.

15

In fundō, Jēmima Kep canem invēnit.

On the farm, Jemima met Kep the collie dog.

16

Eī omnia nārrāvit. Kep statim intellēxit.

She told Kep everything. Kep understood at once.

17

Jēmima herbās ad vulpem portāvit. Ille laetissimus erat.

Jemima brought the herbs to the fox. He was very happy.

18

Sed Kep cum duōbus canibus vēnātōriīs vēnit!

But Kep came with two foxhound dogs!

19

Canēs vulpem fugāvērunt! In silvam fūgit.

The dogs chased the fox away! He fled into the woods.

20

Jēmima salva erat. Sed canēs ōva ēderant.

Jemima was safe. But the dogs had eaten her eggs.

21

Posteā Jēmima quattuor anāticulās ēdūxit. Quattuor tantum.

Later, Jemima hatched four ducklings. Only four.

22
