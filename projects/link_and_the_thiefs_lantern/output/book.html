Link and the Thief's Lantern

Īnsula Windfall multās lucernās habēbat. Omnī nocte lūcēbant.

Windfall Island had many lanterns. They shone every night.

1

Sed ūnō māne — trēs lucernae āberant!

But one morning — three lanterns were gone!

2

Proximā nocte — quīnque plūrēs āberant! Quis lucernās capit?

The next night — five more gone! Who takes the lanterns?

3

Incolae īrātī erant. "Quis lucernās capit?" clāmāvērunt.

The people were angry. "Who takes the lanterns?" they said.

4

"Fūrem invenī!" Lincō dīxērunt. "Eum cape!"

"Find the thief!" they told Link. "Catch him!"

5

Nox vēnit. Lincus tēctum ascendit. Spectāvit.

Night came. Link climbed to a rooftop. He watched.

6

Exspectāvit. Quis lucernās capit?

He waited. Who takes the lanterns?

7

Tum — parva umbra sē mōvit. Lucernam rapuit. Fūgit!

Then — a small shadow moved. It grabbed a lantern. It ran!

8

Lincus dēsiluit. Tacitē secūtus est.

Link jumped down. He followed quietly.

9

Fūr ex oppidō cucurrit. Collem ascendit!

The thief ran out of town. Up the hill!

10

Quis lucernās capit? Moblīnī! Lincus castra exspectāvit.

Who takes the lanterns? Moblins! Link expected a camp.

11

Sed castra nōn erant. Sōlum spelunca. Sōlum ūnus parvus Moblīnus.

But there was no camp. Just a cave. Just one small Moblin.

12

Spelunca plēna lucernārum erat. Omnēs accēnsae. Omnēs lūcēbant.

The cave was full of lanterns. All lit. All glowing.

13

Parvus Moblīnus in mediō sedēbat. Reliquiās edēbat. Sōlus erat.

The little Moblin sat in the middle. He was eating scraps. He was alone.

14

Lincus in ōstiō speluncae stetit. Moblīnus eum vīdit.

Link stood in the cave entrance. The Moblin saw him.

15

Moblīnus faciem tēxit. Tremēbat. Ictum exspectābat.

The Moblin covered his face. He shook. He waited to be hit.

16

Lincus nōn sē mōvit. Moblīnum spectāvit. Lucernās spectāvit.

Link did not move. He looked at the Moblin. He looked at the lanterns.

17

Lincus sēdit.

Link sat down.

18

Ūnā in lūce sēdērunt. Calidum erat.

They sat together in the light. It was warm.

19

Lincus surrēxit. Dīmidium lucernārum cēpit. Moblīnus spectāvit sed nōn fūgit.

Link stood up. He took half the lanterns. The Moblin watched but did not run.

20

Lincus lucernās domum portāvit. In stīpitēs reposuit.

Link carried the lanterns home. He hung them on their posts.

21

"Quis lucernās capit?" rogāvērunt incolae. "Fūr abiit," inquit Lincus.

"Who takes the lanterns?" asked the people. "The thief is gone," said Link.

22

Lincus suam lucernam cēpit. Collem ascendit. Eam ad speluncam relīquit.

Link took his own lantern. He walked up the hill. He left it by the cave.
