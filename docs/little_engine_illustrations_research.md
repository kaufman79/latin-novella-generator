# The Little Engine That Could (1930) — Illustration Research

Research date: 2026-03-28

The 1930 edition (Watty Piper / Lois Lenski) entered the US public domain on January 1, 2026.

---

## Summary of Findings

**We have more than enough illustrations for a picture book.** Between the two Internet Archive scans and the Wikimedia Commons uploads, there are 26 pages of the original 1930 Lenski edition available, plus 15 high-resolution individual illustration scans from the 1949 printing (same Lenski art, better color reproduction). The best strategy is to combine these sources.

---

## Source 1: Internet Archive — High-Resolution B&W Scan

**URL:** https://archive.org/details/the-little-engine-that-could_202512

- **Identifier:** `the-little-engine-that-could_202512`
- **Pages:** 26 (pages 0000-0025 in JP2 zip)
- **Resolution:** ~2640x3367 pixels (very high quality)
- **Color:** Grayscale/B&W only — the scan was done in B&W, so no color information even on the color plates
- **Formats available:** PDF (2.9MB), JP2 ZIP (10MB), EPUB
- **Download:** JP2 ZIP gives individual page images; freely downloadable
- **Quality:** Excellent resolution, sharp line work, but the loss of color is a major limitation

### How to download individual pages:
```bash
curl -O "https://archive.org/download/the-little-engine-that-could_202512/the-little-engine-that-could_jp2.zip"
unzip the-little-engine-that-could_jp2.zip
# Produces: the-little-engine-that-could_0000.jp2 through _0025.jp2
```

## "The Pony Engine" (1910) by Mary C. Jacobs

**URL:** https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The_Pony_Engine

- The original 1910 story that "The Little Engine That Could" was retold from
- Published in *Kindergarten Review* in 1910
- **Very short:** ~400-500 words (much shorter than the 1930 version)
- **No illustrations available.** The original publication had a footnote: "An illustration given in a lecture served as a basis for this little story," but the illustration itself has not been preserved.
- One related image on Wikimedia Commons: `The_Pony_Engine,_by_Mary_C._Jacobs_(1910).png` — 572x714, very small, sourced from Google Books. Appears to be just the title/header from the magazine page, not a story illustration.
- Full text is on Wikisource with audio recording available
- **Not useful for our picture book** — too short and no illustrations

A separate version by Mabel C. Bragg appeared in 1916, also titled "The Pony Engine," which added the kid-friendly cargo elements. No illustrations from that version have been found either.

## Assessment for Latin Picture Book Use

### What we have:
- **7 color plates** from the original Lenski edition (cover + 6 interior color illustrations)
- **~10-12 B&W line drawings** integrated with text pages
- **15 high-resolution color scans** from the 1949 printing (same Lenski art)
- **Full text** available for translation
- **26 total pages** in the original book

### Illustration count for a 20-page Latin book:

The 1949 Wikimedia color scans are the **best primary source**: 13 substantial illustrations + 2 small vignettes = **15 usable images at print quality**. Combined with the cover art, that gives us **16 illustrations**.

For a 20-page book, that means:
- ~16 pages with original Lenski illustrations
- ~4 pages that would need either:
  - Text-only layout (acceptable for some pages)
  - Using B&W illustrations from the high-res IA scan (could work as a stylistic choice)
  - Generated art in Lenski's style (possible but risks visual inconsistency)

### Recommended approach:

**Option A: Use all available Lenski art (RECOMMENDED)**
1. Use the 15 high-res 1949 color scans as primary illustrations
2. Use the 1930 cover art (from Wikimedia or low-res IA color scan)
3. For remaining pages, use B&W line drawings from the high-res IA scan
4. The mix of color plates and B&W pages is *authentic to the original book* — Lenski's edition alternated between them
5. This gives 20+ illustrations total, enough for every page

**Option B: Color only, supplement with generated art**
1. Use the 15 color scans + cover = 16 color images
2. Generate 4-5 additional illustrations using Gemini in Lenski's style
3. Risk: generated art may not match Lenski's distinctive 1930s style

**Option C: Shorter book**
1. Use only the 15 color illustrations
2. Condense the Latin translation to match the illustration count
3. Results in a ~15-page book, which is within our 15-30 page target
